THE ISSUE

There have been setbacks in the funding of their research and conflict from conservatism communities. A lack of funding means less chances to move forward with the de-extinction of the Tasmanian Tiger.

$5.2b is set for Agriculture, forestry and fishing. $224.5m of the budget is allocated to projects and research regarding Threatened Species, which is already catered to 1,900 animals. Research suggests Australia must spend $2 billion a year to save its 1,900 most-imperilled species. Hence, there's not enough funding.

What can you do to help?

  • Sign the petition to get more funding supported by the government
  • Follow news from Colossal Biosciences
  • Follow news from the University of Melbourne who are establishing the Thylacine Integrated Genetic Restoration Research (TIGRR) Lab.
